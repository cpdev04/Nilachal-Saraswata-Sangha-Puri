"Jayaguru"

Sri Sri Nigamananda Saraswati Dev's life is a testament to the power of faith, perseverance, and the quest for truth. His teachings encourage us to embrace love, wisdom, and the light of motherhood, reminding us to honour our spiritual paths and the bonds we share with one another.

By understanding that we are all part of the same divine essence, we can cultivate compassion and unity in our lives. His philosophy inspires us to look beyond our differences and recognize the inherent divinity in every being, guiding us towards a deeper connection with ourselves and the universe.

The Philosophy of Paramahansa Sri Nigamananda Saraswati Deva

TParamahansa Sri Sri Nigamananda Saraswati Deva followed the teachings of Sankaracharya, i.e., Advaita Vedanta. This philosophy says that there is only one Brahman, and everything, including all living beings and the universe, is part of Brahman. They are not separate from it.

  • Brahman is everywhere and is one, which is called Advaita Brahmavad.
  • Nature (Prakriti) is diverse but not a single entity.
  • From Brahman, Maya appeared, which has three qualities: Sattva (goodness), Rajas (passion), and Tamas (ignorance). From Maya came space (Akasha), then air, followed by fire, water, and finally earth, all in very small forms.
  • When living beings connect with Brahman, they break free from the cycle of birth and death. This leads to lasting peace, known as Brahma Nirvana, which can only be achieved through the kindness of a Sadguru.
